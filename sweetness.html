Sweetness

Sweetness was designed as a responsive app and e-commerce store that allows customers to easily navigate and filter through the extensive Sweetness inventory and reduce the amount of time it takes for a customer to select their desired sweet. With a vast array of sweets offered, many customers became overwhelmed and quickly finalized their order, resulting in a reduced quantity of items purchased in a single transaction. This meant developing smart filtering options that were easy to navigate whilst maintaining the aesthetic design of the brand.

I was the acting UI/UX Designer and I created a user flow diagram, wireframes, branding, mockups and preformed user testing. This piece took one month to complete, June of 2021 and Sketch was utilized.

My Process

User Flow

Three user stories were used to create a User Flow Diagram; a new customer wants advanced filtering options, place multiple items in a shopping cart, so that they can purchase more than one item at a time, and they want to access the inventory without having to register. A user flow diagram was created to detail the number of screens needed to accomodate these tasks.

Sketches

Based on the user flow, screens were promptly sketched and then digitized to Mid-fidelity Wireframes, a prototype was created and sent out to test the user flow. High-Fidelity wireframes exhibited the addition of user testing input.

Branding

Sweetness was in need of a rebrand. The look of the app and website sought to create a nostalgic feel. Soft pastel pinks and greens were selected to accommodate the old fashioned candy look. A text logo was created and the image of the candy icon was inspired by the easy to recognise iconic lollipop.

Design

The branding guidelines created for Sweetness, set the stage for the Sweetness app and website. The high-fidelity wireframes were a culmination of user testing, feedback, and brand guidelines.

Prototype

Users were tasked to add two Sugar Free Root Beer Gummies to their cart and checkout.

The final prototype was modified per the user suggestions offering an even simpler and advanced way to filter certain candies, add items to the cart in multiple ways, and while checking out the user can decide to make an account or continue to place their order without making an account.

View the Prototype.

Conclusion

A responsive app was successfully created allowing for the user to easily and quickly filter their favorite sweets. After user testing, the application was further honed to simplify the process of ordering multiple products. Sweetness accomplished it’s goals to allow the ability to filter through the mass amount of candy inventory, order multiple items, all while not having to create an account. Sweetness also adhered to the brand guidelines and the app and website are cohesive as a result.
